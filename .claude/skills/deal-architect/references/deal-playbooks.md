# Deal Playbooks — Prometheus iQ
Three deal architectures specific to Prometheus iQ's context. Each defines the structure, the power dynamics, and the minimum/ideal/walk-away terms.
---
## Playbook 1: Sales Deal (Closing an STR Operator)
**Context:** Gian is negotiating with an STR property manager (typically 10–65 units) who has been through some portion of the qualification and discovery process. The prospect has been identified in the Leads or Deals list in ClickUp. Pull their data from the sales-assistant skill before applying this playbook.
### Power Structure
- Prometheus iQ needs revenue; the prospect needs better revenue performance
- Leverage tilts toward Prometheus iQ if: the prospect has already invested time in the process (demos, calls), they've expressed pain about current pricing decisions, or they have a high property count (more to lose from bad pricing)
- Leverage tilts toward the prospect if: they're price shopping, they have an easy alternative (competitor or manual process they're comfortable with), or they haven't experienced a clear pain point yet
### The Framing That Works
Don't sell features. Sell the one moment that hurts them most: "You priced that mountain cabin at $180 on a weekend in February when the market was at $290. That's $110 per night, per property, gone. At 20 properties, that's a $2,200 weekend you can't get back." Make the loss vivid before the subscription price ever enters the conversation.
### Term Architecture
| Element | Ideal | Minimum Acceptable | Walk-Away |
|---|---|---|---|
| Price | $399/mo (full rate) | $299/mo introductory | Below $249/mo for 6+ months |
| Contract term | Annual (12 months prepaid) | Monthly (with upgrade path) | Month-to-month with no commitment |
| Onboarding | Structured 30-day onboarding commitment from prospect | Self-serve with check-in at day 15 | No engagement plan |
| Expansion | Verbal commitment to expand if X units added | None required | — |
### Closing Triggers to Build In
- Offer an onboarding date as the anchor: "We can get you live before your peak season if we start by [date]." This makes the cost of delay concrete.
- For high-value prospects (ARR > $15K), offer a 30-day pilot with a clear success metric agreed upfront — operators who define success before the pilot almost always convert.
### Objection-Specific Moves
- **"Too expensive"** → Anchor the ROI: "If we recover one lost weekend of pricing for you, that covers 3 months of subscription. We typically see 15–20% revenue uplift in the first 90 days."
- **"Not the right time"** → Set a future date and send a calendar invite: "Let's talk again the week before your peak season. I'll pull together your market's pricing data so we have context for the conversation."
- **"Need to think about it"** → Ask the one question: "What would need to be true for this to be a clear yes?" This surfaces the real objection.

## Playbook 2: Partnership Deal (PMS Integration / Distribution)
**Context:** Prometheus iQ is negotiating with a Property Management System (PMS), marketplace, or distribution platform to create a data integration or co-marketing relationship. The goal is access to their operator base, their data layer, or their platform real estate — not a direct sale.
**Known active conversations:** Hostaway (contact: Marcus Rader — white-label or integration), PMS outreach (multiple companies), Airbnb (one-pager complete — relationship building).
### Power Structure
This is the most complex deal type. The PMS holds:
- Distribution (access to their operators)
- Data (property and booking data Prometheus iQ needs)
- Attention (operators trust their PMS recommendation)
Prometheus iQ holds:
- Revenue intelligence (something their operators demonstrably want)
- Speed (building this internally would take the PMS 6–12 months)
- Operator insight (deep understanding of STR revenue pain points)
**The core framing:** This is not a vendor relationship. This is a capability they can't easily replicate, offered in a form that makes them look better to their operators. Frame it as: "We make your operators more successful. Successful operators don't churn. This is a retention play for you."
### Entering the Conversation
Never approach a PMS cold with a feature pitch. Approach with a question about their operators: "We've been working with operators on your platform. We're seeing a consistent pattern — operators managing 20+ properties are leaving 15–25% of revenue on the table in peak periods. Is that something you're hearing from your customers?" Let them express the problem before you offer the solution.
### Term Architecture
| Element | Ideal | Minimum Acceptable | Walk-Away |
|---|---|---|---|
| Integration type | White-label embed inside PMS dashboard | Native API integration + preferred partner listing | API access only, no commercial relationship |
| Revenue arrangement | Revenue share on referred customers (15–20% MRR) | Co-marketing agreement (no direct revenue share) | No commercial arrangement whatsoever |
| Data access | Bidirectional: Prometheus iQ accesses booking/property data + writes pricing recommendations back | Read access to property data for intelligence layer | No data access — integration impossible |
| Exclusivity | None (avoid at all costs without significant payment) | Preferred status in their marketplace (non-exclusive) | Exclusive arrangement for any partner |
| Term | 2-year agreement with renewal | 1-year with 90-day exit | Month-to-month (not worth engineering investment) |
### The Hostaway Play Specifically
Marcus Rader is Hostaway's CEO/co-founder. He's a builder and operator, not just a business developer. The entry point that works with founders: technical depth + operator empathy, not sales polish. The pitch is a conversation between builders: "Here's what we're seeing in operator revenue data. Here's the gap. Here's what we've built. I'd love your read on whether this creates value for your operators."
Don't send a deck first. Request a 20-minute call. Leads with curiosity, not a proposal.
### The Competition-as-Urgency Tactic
Once one PMS integration is in progress (even in preliminary discussions), every other PMS conversation gets easier. "We're currently in integration discussions with [PMS X]" is not a threat — it's information. It activates Law 17 (unpredictability) and the ripening effect simultaneously. Use it.

## Playbook 3: Fundraising Deal (Investor Negotiation)
**Context:** Prometheus iQ is raising capital — either a seed round, a bridge, or a strategic round. Before any investor conversation, pull current financials from the CFO skill: cash balance, monthly burn rate, and runway. **Never enter an investor conversation without knowing these three numbers.**
### Power Structure
The investor holds capital. Prometheus iQ holds opportunity. Leverage is almost entirely determined by one variable: **how many credible investors are in the process simultaneously.**
One investor = they control the negotiation.
Three investors = you control the negotiation.
The entire fundraising strategy should be structured to create investor simultaneity before the first term sheet is discussed.
### The Frame That Commands Respect
Investors fund momentum, not need. Even if you need the capital urgently, the conversation should sound like you're being selective: "We're having early conversations with a few investors who understand the STR market deeply. We're not looking for the largest check — we're looking for the right partner for this phase." This is Law 6 (make others come to you) in action.
### The Three Numbers You Need Before Any Call
Pull these from Mercury via the CFO skill before the meeting:
1. **Current cash balance** — know it exactly, to the dollar
2. **Monthly burn rate** — 3-month average, not last month
3. **Runway at current burn** — this determines your negotiating urgency level
If runway > 12 months: negotiate from strength — you don't need this check
If runway 6–12 months: negotiate from stability — you're raising to accelerate, not survive
If runway < 6 months: your leverage is weakest — either fix this before fundraising, or use the urgency strategically (some investors respond to "we're moving fast and the round is filling")
### Term Architecture
| Element | Ideal | Minimum Acceptable | Walk-Away |
|---|---|---|---|
| Valuation | Pre-money based on 3–5x current ARR run rate or comparable SaaS multiples | Slight discount for lead investor check size | Valuation that represents greater than 30% dilution in a single round |
| Check size | Lead investor: 50–60% of round target | Any amount that establishes a credible lead | Commitments so small they don't close the round |
| Board seat | No board seat for seed | Observer seat only | Full board seat with veto rights at seed |
| Pro-rata rights | Acceptable if investor earns it | Standard pro-rata on their initial check | Super pro-rata at favorable terms |
| Exclusivity / lock-up | None | None | No exclusive negotiating period over 30 days |
### The FOMO Activation Play
At the right moment — typically when you have 40–60% of the round committed — make the scarcity real: "We're moving toward close and have room for one or two more investors. If you want to get in at these terms, the window is the next two weeks." This is not dishonest — it's structuring the decision environment. The investor either moves or they miss it.
### Strategic Investor vs. Capital Investor
For Prometheus iQ's distribution-dependent model, a strategic investor (a PMS company, a large STR operator group, or a hospitality tech fund) may be worth more than an equivalent check from a pure financial investor. Consider: does this investor open doors to the partnership conversations in Playbook 2? If yes, their check is worth a higher valuation premium.

## Cross-Playbook Principles (Apply to All Three)
**Never show the full hand.** In every negotiation, hold something back — a feature, a concession, a timeline flexibility — that you can offer later if needed. Offering everything upfront eliminates your ability to trade.
**The first yes is a staging yes.** The other party's first "this is interesting" or "let's move forward" is not a commitment. It's an opening. Treat it as such — keep the conversation moving without releasing pressure.
**Exit gracefully.** If a deal is going nowhere, close it cleanly. "This doesn't seem like the right fit at this stage — let's stay in touch and revisit when [condition]." Burning a bridge is costly. A clean exit often opens future leverage (they remember that you were gracious).
